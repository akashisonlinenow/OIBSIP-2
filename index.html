Kalpana Chawla

1962 - 2003

First Woman Space Traveller

Kalpana Chawla

Kalpana Chawla, an iconic figure in the realm of space exploration, was born on March 17, 1962, in Karnal, India. A brilliant aerospace engineer and an accomplished astronaut, Chawla etched her name in history as the first woman of Indian origin in space. Her journey to the stars began when she joined NASA and eventually became a mission specialist on the Space Shuttle Columbia. Notably, she made history as the first Indian-born woman in space during the STS-87 mission in 1997.


Kalpana Chawla's contributions to space exploration extended beyond her groundbreaking achievements. She exemplified resilience and dedication, overcoming challenges and breaking gender and cultural barriers in the field of aerospace. Tragically, her second spaceflight, STS-107, ended in disaster when the Columbia disintegrated upon re-entry in 2003. The world mourned the loss of a courageous astronaut who had inspired millions with her determination and passion for space exploration.


Chawla's legacy endures through scholarships, awards, and institutions named in her honor, emphasizing her lasting impact on the scientific community and serving as an inspiration for future generations of space enthusiasts. Kalpana Chawla's life is a testament to the pursuit of dreams, the spirit of exploration, and the resilience needed to reach for the stars.


Achievements

  • First Woman of Indian Origin in Space: Kalpana Chawla made history in 1997 by becoming the first woman of Indian origin in space during the STS-87 mission aboard the Space Shuttle Columbia.
  • Aerospace Engineer: Chawla earned a Master of Science degree in Aerospace Engineering from the University of Texas at Arlington in 1984 and a Doctorate in Aerospace Engineering from the University of Colorado in 1988.
  • NASA Research Scientist: Before becoming an astronaut, Chawla worked as a research scientist at NASA's Ames Research Center, where she focused on aerodynamics of aircraft.
  • Pilot's License: Kalpana Chawla held a Commercial Pilot's License for single and multi-engine airplanes, seaplanes, and gliders. Her passion for flying was evident in her pursuits beyond her career as an astronaut.
  • First Indian-American Woman in Space: Chawla was the first woman of Indian descent in space and the second Indian-American astronaut overall, following Rakesh Sharma.
  • Multiple Spaceflights: Kalpana Chawla flew on two space missions. Besides STS-87 in 1997, she was a mission specialist on STS-107, which tragically ended in the Columbia disaster in 2003.
  • Contributions to Scientific Research: During her space missions, Chawla conducted various experiments and contributed to scientific research in microgravity, materials science, and atmospheric studies.
  • Inspiration to Others: Chawla's achievements and her journey from a small town in India to space served as an inspiration to aspiring scientists, engineers, and women globally.
